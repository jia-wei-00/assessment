Squad composition in Apex Legends refers to the selection of three Legends that complement each other's abilities to form an effective team. A well-composed squad covers offensive capability, mobility or repositioning, and healing or defensive utility.

A classic beginner-friendly composition is Bloodhound, Bangalore, and Lifeline. Bloodhound provides tracking information about enemy positions and can chase with Beast of the Hunt. Bangalore provides smoke cover for safe revives and disengagement. Lifeline provides passive healing, faster revives, and a Care Package for gear. This composition handles information, escape, and sustain together.

A high-mobility aggressive composition often includes Octane, Horizon, and Caustic or Wattson. Octane's Launch Pad allows the squad to reposition quickly or push enemies from unexpected angles. Horizon adds vertical mobility and area denial with Black Hole. The third legend provides either gas traps for defence after a push or Tesla fences to lock down a final location.

Defensive or ranked-oriented compositions often favour Wattson, Newcastle or Lifeline, and a recon or mobility legend like Bloodhound or Valkyrie. Wattson's Interception Pylon blocks incoming grenades and ultimates while her fences slow and damage enemies trying to enter a building. Newcastle can protect teammates while reviving them and create mobile shields. Valkyrie provides the squad with a full repositioning ultimate if the final ring forces them to move.

Communication is the most important part of squad play. The ping system allows players to mark enemies, locations, weapons, and items without voice chat. Double-tapping the ping button marks a location as danger, alerting teammates to threats. Pinging a door marks it as a suggested entry or exit. Pinging a teammate's deathbox requests that they be revived. Using the ping system consistently allows coordinated play even with random squadmates.

Respawning eliminated teammates is a priority in competitive play. A squad's banner card is left in the teammate's deathbox and must be collected before the timer expires, which starts at 90 seconds after death. The banner can then be taken to a Respawn Beacon on the map to bring the teammate back into the match with no gear, requiring them to loot again. Lifeline's Care Package can also respawn eliminated teammates without requiring a Respawn Beacon on higher-tier versions of her kit.

Focus fire or target prioritisation means the entire squad concentrates damage on one enemy at a time. Spreading damage across multiple enemies gives all opponents time to heal and repositon. Eliminating one enemy immediately creates a numbers advantage, forces the surviving enemies to play more cautiously, and allows the squad to loot a deathbox for supplies.

Holding doors and windows in buildings is a core defensive technique during late-game rings. Wattson fences placed on doorways or windows deal damage and create visible alerts when an enemy crosses them. Caustic traps placed inside doorways trigger when enemies enter, creating a strong layer of denial. A squad holding a fortified building in the final ring zone has a structural advantage over a squad attacking from outside.

The shotgun and SMG close-range combination is one of the most popular loadout pairings. A Mastiff or Peacekeeper paired with an R-99 or R-301 covers both burst damage on the first shot and sustained fire during a push. This combination is effective in buildings and close-quarters environments. For mid to long range, a marksman rifle or sniper paired with an assault rifle provides flexibility across all engagement distances.
