New players to Apex Legends should start by learning the ping system before anything else. Pinging is done by pressing the middle mouse button on PC or the right bumper on console. Pressing ping once marks a location. Pressing it twice quickly marks danger. Holding the ping button opens a contextual ping menu. The ping system allows full team communication without voice chat and is essential for coordinating with random teammates.

Choose a simple legend for your first matches. Bloodhound, Bangalore, and Lifeline are recommended starting legends. Bloodhound's Eye of the Allfather scans for nearby enemies through walls and is easy to use. Bangalore's smoke grenades provide escape cover when you are downed or taking heavy fire. Lifeline automatically revives teammates faster and her Care Package provides useful equipment. Avoid complex legends like Caustic or Crypto until you understand the game flow.

Prioritise gear over kills when you first land. Finding a weapon, Shield, and Helmet should be the immediate goal in the first minute of a match. Without at least a white Shield, a single enemy can knock you immediately. The R-301, R-99, and Flatline are the most reliable weapons to look for when starting out because their recoil is manageable and they are effective in most situations.

Learn to always loot deathboxes after winning a fight. Deathboxes contain all the items that the eliminated player was carrying. After eliminating an enemy, quickly looting their box can provide better shields, more ammo, or upgraded attachments. However, looting in an exposed position during active combat makes you vulnerable, so always move to cover first.

Stick with your teammates at all times. Apex Legends is a squad-based game and solo play is heavily punished. Moving away from your squad leaves you outnumbered in almost every situation. The respawn system means your squad can bring you back if you are eliminated, but only if they survive and collect your banner in time. Always follow the group and never engage fights alone unless your teammates are right behind you.

Manage your ammo carefully. Carrying 200 rounds of Light Ammo and 200 rounds of Heavy Ammo is typically enough for most fights. Carrying excessive ammo wastes inventory slots that could hold healing items. A good rule is to carry two shield batteries, four shield cells, two med kits, and four syringes alongside your ammo and grenades.

Crouch before firing to reduce weapon recoil and improve accuracy. Weapons in Apex Legends have significant vertical and horizontal recoil that must be learned over time. Crouching reduces the effective recoil and makes your hitbox smaller. Avoid standing still in open areas because you become an easy target for enemy snipers and long-range weapons.

The ring damage in early phases is slow and survivable. Do not panic if you find yourself slightly outside the Ring during looting. Small heals can counter the early ring damage while you finish securing gear before rotating. In later ring phases, the damage becomes lethal within seconds, so always leave enough time to rotate safely before the Ring fully closes.

Grenades are highly effective and often underused by new players. Frag grenades can be bounced off walls into windows and around corners. Thermite grenades create a line of fire that denies movement and deals continuous damage. Arc Stars stick to surfaces and enemies before exploding and deal additional damage through shields. Carrying one or two grenades provides additional options during fights and when flushing enemies out of buildings.

Watch kill feeds and audio cues to gather information. The kill feed in the top right corner shows when squads are eliminated, which tells you how many teams remain and where fights are happening. Gunshots heard in the distance indicate nearby squads. A red directional indicator on your screen shows where incoming damage is coming from. Paying attention to these cues helps you make better decisions about when to push, hold, or rotate away from danger.
